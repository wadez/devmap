History of Hypertext

Dad says history is important. Without history, we are doomed to repeat our selves. You should know that HTML stands for Hyper Text Markup Language. Understanding HTML will allow you to turn text into something spectactular. Dad says scientists use HTML to share notes on topics like Space, Trucks, and Dinosaurs!

Back in the day, everyone was hyper about the internet. After all, the internet was created by Army soldiers. Dad says "Hyper" was the trend word because it meant "beyond". Hyper text was beyond books and reports. It was beyond cool. Anyone in the world with your address could access your text. Neato!

So What

With HTML, you can do anything a typewriter or typing software can do. You can create headings, italics, bold, underline, etc. That's lame! What is really cool is anchoring your page to someone else's page. With a click of the mouse you can teleport to somewhere else on the World Wide Web (WWW)!

The anchoring feature is perfect for referencing other documents. Dad says they should use this in the sales department. You can anchor images, celebrity documents, and other totally awesome stuff!

Mom says you can make a web page for a school report and your teacher will gasp in astonishment! They will likely just hand you and "A+" and a sticker! So what do you say? Do you want to join the cool club of web developers?

This report took forever! I spent 15 minutes on it! Guess what?! Learn to HTML code only takes 5 seconds! Just make a file called report.html on your computer.

Type your report into the report.html file. Then put tags around words. My dad uses the HTML cheat sheet below.

Tag purpose
<h1> a big title
<h3> a smaller title
<b> bold
<a> anchor to another page
<img> show an image

Now your report writing looks something like this!
